## Objective
Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage when writing or speaking.

## Essential Question
How do verbs and nouns work together to create meaning in sentences?

## Instructional Materials
- Whiteboard and markers
- Chart paper and markers
- Printed handouts with sentences for practice
- Projector and screen for displaying examples
- Index cards with verbs and nouns

## Teaching Strategies
- Think-Pair-Share: Students will discuss with a partner to deepen understanding.
- Interactive Modeling: Teacher will model identifying verbs and nouns using examples.
- Collaborative Learning: Students will work in small groups to practice skills.

## Hook (5 min)
Begin with a quick game of "Verb and Noun Charades" where students act out a verb paired with a noun that the class has to guess. For example, "eating cake" or "running dog."

## Direct Instruction (10 min)
1. Explain what verbs and nouns are, using the whiteboard to write definitions and examples.
2. Model identifying verbs and nouns in a sentence. Example: "The cat (noun) sleeps (verb) on the mat (noun)."
3. Use the projector to display sentences and highlight verbs and nouns in different colors.

## Guided Practice (10 min)
1. Divide students into small groups and distribute index cards with verbs and nouns.
2. Each group will create sentences using the cards and write them on chart paper.
3. Groups will share their sentences, identifying the verbs and nouns in each.

## Independent Practice (10 min)
Provide students with a handout containing sentences. Students will identify and underline the verbs and circle the nouns independently. Success criteria: correctly identify at least 80% of the verbs and nouns.

## Assessment (5 min)
Write a sentence using at least one verb and one noun. Underline the verb and circle the noun.

## Assignments
- Completed handout with identified verbs and nouns
- Written sentence with verb and noun highlighted

## Homework Notes
- Practice identifying verbs and nouns in a paragraph from a book you are reading at home.
- Discuss with a family member how verbs and nouns help in storytelling.

## Differentiation
- For ELL students: Provide visual aids and sentence starters to support understanding. Pair with a buddy for guided practice.
- For the student with IEP: Allow additional time for independent practice and provide a simplified handout with fewer sentences.

## Teacher Reflection
- Were students able to identify verbs and nouns accurately?
- How did the ELL students engage with the material, and what adjustments could support them further?
- Did the charades game effectively energize and focus the students for the lesson?

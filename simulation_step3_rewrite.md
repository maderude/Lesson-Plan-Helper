## Objective
Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage by constructing sentences that correctly use a variety of grammatical structures, including verbs and nouns.

## Essential Question
How do verbs and nouns work together to create clear and effective sentences?

## Instructional Materials
- Whiteboard and markers
- Chart paper
- Sentence strips
- Handout with sentences missing verbs or nouns
- Laptops or tablets for digital sentence-building activity

## Teaching Strategies
- Direct Instruction: Explicit teaching of nouns and verbs with examples.
- Collaborative Learning: Students work in pairs to identify and use verbs and nouns.
- Technology Integration: Using a digital tool for interactive sentence-building.

## Hook (5 min)
Begin with a quick game of "Word Toss": Toss a soft ball to a student and say a noun; the student tosses it back with a verb that could logically go with the noun. Repeat with several students.

## Direct Instruction (10 min)
Explain the roles of verbs and nouns in sentences. Write examples on the board, highlighting verbs in one color and nouns in another. For instance, "The cat (noun) jumps (verb) over the fence (noun)." Provide a few more examples and discuss how verbs show action or state of being, while nouns represent people, places, things, or ideas.

## Guided Practice (10 min)
In pairs, students receive sentence strips with either a noun or a verb missing. They need to choose from a list of words to complete each sentence accurately. Walk around to facilitate and provide feedback. Share some completed sentences with the class.

## Independent Practice (10 min)
Students individually complete a worksheet with sentences that have blanks for either a noun or a verb. They must fill in the blanks using a word bank provided. Success criteria: Correctly identify and place at least 8 out of 10 verbs and nouns.

## Assessment (5 min)
Write a short paragraph using at least five nouns and five verbs. Highlight the verbs in one color and the nouns in another. This will be collected for assessment.

## Assignments
- Completed Guided Practice sentence strips
- Independent Practice worksheet
- Highlighted paragraph from the assessment

## Homework Notes
- Identify five verbs and five nouns from a book or article read at home.
- Practice using each identified verb in a new sentence.

## Differentiation
- ELL students: Provide bilingual word banks and visual aids depicting actions for verbs and items for nouns.
- Student with IEP: Allow extended time and provide sentence strips with more contextual clues. Offer one-on-one support during independent practice.

## Teacher Reflection
- Were students able to identify and use verbs and nouns with ease?
- Did the strategies used effectively engage all students, including ELL and those with IEPs?
- How can I modify the lesson for students who found the material either too challenging or too easy?
